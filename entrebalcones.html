Entre Balcones

PVR Experience| 2025

A VR - Hand Tracked journey through community stories, photography, and the living memory of a Colombian neighborhood

Entre Balcones is a hand-tracked VR experience built with Unity and the Meta SDK. Set in a balcony overlooking the neighborhood of Carlos E, users explore memories and local stories by interacting with floating menus, hand gestures, and an immersive photo-taking mechanic.

From the central balcony (lobby), players can teleport to two locations: Don Miguel’s store and the green lung. In each, characters share memories, and players must photograph glowing objects using a virtual camera. These images are stored in a floating wrist menu, where users can check objectives and build a photo collage by scaling, placing, and snapping their photos into an interactive album.

.

About the project

This project was developed by a team of five people: three designers and two programmers. I led the VR side, while my teammate focused on a parallel augmented reality experience.

Our goal was to create an interactive memory archive of the neighborhood. For example, one scene recreates the night when neighbors planted trees to protect a local park. By documenting these stories in VR, we invite users to reflect on memory, place, and resistance.

My role in the development

  • Developed the entire VR experience with hand tracking using the Meta SDK.
  • Implemented all core mechanics: gesture-based UI, virtual camera, objective system, and final photo collage interaction.
  • Scripted and controlled character animations and interaction timing inside Unity.
  • Integrated all visual elements and transitions designed by the rest of the team.

What I took away from this project

I gained deep experience working with the Meta Oculus SDK, including editing and implementing building blocks for custom hand gestures, floating UI menus, and intuitive VR interaction flows.

This project also helped me strengthen my understanding of UX/UI design for immersive environments and how to make VR interactions natural and accessible to first-time users.
