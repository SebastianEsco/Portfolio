Nuclear Rush

PC Videogame | 2024

A fast-paced transformation-based runner set inside a chaotic nuclear meltdown

Nuclear Rush Nuclear Rush is a fast-paced 2.5D endless runner developed in Unity, where players control a factory worker escaping a nuclear plant moments before its explosion. As the player advances, they gather radioactive pickups to transform into different mutated forms—each with unique movement mechanics—allowing them to survive increasingly complex obstacles in a chaotic, stylized environment.

About the project

Nuclear Rush is a stylized 3D endless runner developed in Unity URP over the course of three months as part of a fifth-semester academic project. The game features a 2.5D layout where players stay fixed on screen while the world moves around them, racing through a collapsing nuclear facility. Players collect radioactive substances to transform between three unique forms: a human who jumps and slides, a tortuaraña that clings to floors and ceilings by reversing gravity, and a mutated bird that flies in bursts like in Flappy Bird. Each transformation is essential to overcoming different hazards, from toxic puddles and debris to anti-radiation masks that reduce your mutation energy. The ultimate goal is to survive all three phases and escape the facility before it explodes. Despite the fast-paced and chaotic tone, the game includes a structured progression system with score tracking, upgrades, and a final win condition. Players earn points based on pickups and distance, which can be used in a store to improve speed and extend the time before the explosion. Visual feedback is reinforced through animations, sounds, and a dynamic camera that enhances key gameplay moments.

The game was well received during its showcase, with players engaging repeatedly to beat their previous runs and complete the full transformation cycle. The project’s main challenge was balancing visual appeal and responsive gameplay while keeping performance optimized—a task that was tackled through object pooling, smart scene management, and careful use of URP post-processing.

My role in the development

As one of two developers, I was responsible for the core systems that shaped the player experience. I implemented all movement mechanics across the three transformation states, built the procedural level generation system, and handled player stats persistence between scenes. I also developed the main menu, in-game store, dynamic camera behavior, and integrated textures, animations, and audio. Beyond functionality, I focused on optimization—using object pooling, efficient off-screen object management, and scalable code architecture. T his was my first time working with Unity in 3D, and it was a valuable learning experience in clean coding practices, system modularity, and visual polish. I'm proud of how fun and visually engaging the final result turned out to be, and of the strong player engagement we saw during live testing.

What I took away from this project

Nuclear Rush marked my first full experience developing a 3D game in Unity, and it pushed me to grow both technically and creatively. I learned how to structure scalable and clean code for systems involving movement, procedural generation, and UI. Implementing optimization techniques like object pooling and off-screen cleanup gave me a strong foundation in performance-aware design. Working in a small team taught me how to organize tasks effectively, communicate clearly, and build flexible systems that could adapt quickly to change. I also gained practical knowledge of URP, lighting, and camera design to support a visually dynamic experience. Seeing players genuinely enjoy the game—and return to replay it multiple times—was deeply rewarding and confirmed the value of combining playful mechanics with strong technical execution.
